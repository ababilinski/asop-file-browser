Privacy Policy

Your files stay local.

No account. No app analytics. No cloud transfer.

Effective July 11, 2026

What reaches us

Nothing from the app. ASOP File Browser does not create an account, send telemetry to Adrian Babilinski, or upload your phone’s files. It has no ads or analytics.

Files and device information

The app shows device names, connection status, storage information, file listings, and app details from the connected phone. This information stays on your Mac. File transfers go directly between your Mac and phone over USB or your local network.

The app only changes files, apps, or phone settings when you choose an action. It does not send device or file information to Adrian Babilinski.

Recoverable Trash keeps a local record on your Mac so deleted files can be restored. The record can include the phone serial number, original and Trash paths, filename, deletion date, and file size.

Copies stored on your Mac

Previews, thumbnails, screenshots, recordings, drag-and-drop exports, and cross-device transfers can create local copies or staging files on your Mac. Saved downloads remain where you chose to put them. Cached and staged copies may remain until you clear them manually, remove the app’s cache, or macOS removes temporary files.

If you choose Managed Copy and Download in Settings, the app downloads the official scrcpy package from GitHub, verifies it, and stores it in Application Support. You can remove the managed copy from Settings. See Phone Tools downloads and licenses.

scrcpy startup output and similar diagnostic logs may be written to temporary local files when a tool fails. They are not sent to the publisher automatically.

Settings

Preferences such as appearance, favorite locations, connection checks, and selected tool paths are stored locally in your macOS user account. Removing the app does not always remove these preferences automatically.

USB, Wi-Fi, and Phone Tools

Developer features use ADB, and Phone Control and other Phone Tools also use scrcpy. These tools run locally and communicate directly with your phone over USB or your local network. Wi-Fi pairing and file operations can send device information and file contents between your Mac and phone. The app does not route that traffic through an ASOP File Browser server.

See their official downloads, source links, and licenses in Third-Party Notices.

Phone Tools download

The app only contacts GitHub for Phone Tools after you choose Download, Update, or Repair. GitHub receives the normal information needed to serve a download, such as your IP address and request details. ASOP File Browser does not upload your files, phone information, or tool logs with that request.

The website

This website has no publisher-operated analytics, advertising, account system, or contact form. It is hosted by GitHub Pages, which logs visitor IP addresses for security and may process browser requests under the GitHub Pages data-collection terms and GitHub General Privacy Statement.

Following an outside link moves you to that provider’s website and privacy policy.

We do not sell app data

Your files and app activity are not sent to us, and we do not sell them.

Your choices

  • Use File Transfer Mode without enabling Developer Options.
  • Use USB instead of Wi-Fi debugging.
  • Choose where downloads, screenshots, and recordings are saved.
  • Use the cache controls in Settings, and delete saved, cached, or staged files from your Mac when you no longer need them.
  • Revoke USB or Wi-Fi debugging authorizations from Android Settings.

Changes and questions

This policy may change when the app’s data handling changes. The effective date above will be updated. Questions can be opened through the project’s GitHub issues. Issues are public, so do not include personal information or device data.
